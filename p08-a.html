Washington Crosses the Delaware
American Wing · Gallery 760 · 2nd Floor
◈

This painting may be the most successful propaganda piece ever produced. It was made 70 years after the actual crossing, by an artist who was not there. Almost nothing in it is accurate: the true boats used were much larger, the American flag did not yet exist, and most importantly, take a look at the actual distance they crossed. It's not exactly a mission impossible. This painting is not history — it is mythology and propaganda. It was designed to make Washington look like a hero.
