The Bank Facade
American Wing · Gallery 700 · 2nd Floor
◈

Beyond the repeating triangular caps to the main building and windows, the Masonic influence can mainly be seen in the entrance. The main door is located in between two columns which are capped with an arch. Within that arch is a window. What seems simple has deeper meaning — the Masons would refer to this doorway as a portal and the window above as an eye. There is a famous eye we associate with banking — carried around in our pockets.

This symbol is called the Eye of Providence and is supposed to represent the eye of God. It frequently features rays emanating from it. These rays are right here on this bank facade window. The tie between these Freemason symbols and the banking system is right here.
