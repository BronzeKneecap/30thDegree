Cleopatra's Needle
Central Park · East Side

In 1880, over 9,000 Freemasons marched 3.4 miles through these streets to mark this obelisk's installation. It was the largest Masonic public display in American history. The Obelisk is said to be created in 1425 BC in Egypt. It is carved out of granite from Aswan, the same quarry used by the pyramids. While the ancient Egyptian intentions are not fully known, today the obelisk stands as a Masonic symbol: a pillar reaching from earth toward the divine, the same form that appears in every Masonic lodge as a symbol of hidden knowledge reaching upward. During the 1880 procession, the Grand Marshal stood at each side of the obelisk, clapped 3 times, and repeated the mantra "in ample form" 3 times.
